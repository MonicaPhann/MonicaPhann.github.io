Website Evaluation

Google.com

Google’s website is iconic for its extreme simplicity and focus on search functionality. It uses a clean design with minimal distractions, allowing users to focus on their search queries.

  • Contrast: Google uses strong contrast with a stark white background and its colorful logo and dark text, making the search bar the clear focal point.
  • Repetition: The site uses consistent fonts, button styles, and layouts across pages, reinforcing usability and familiarity.
  • Alignment: All elements are centrally aligned, creating a balanced and uncluttered layout that draws the user’s eye to the search bar.
  • Proximity: Elements like the search bar and buttons are grouped logically, maintaining a clean and intuitive structure that supports quick interaction.

Craigslist.com

Craigslist’s website is famous (or infamous) for its ultra-minimal, barebones design that prioritizes function over aesthetics.

  • Contrast: Craigslist has very low visual contrast. Most of the text is the same size and color, with minimal use of color or visual hierarchy to distinguish important elements.
  • Repetition: Repetition exists, but mostly because the design hasn’t changed in decades. Fonts, links, and layout are consistent—though that consistency feels more like stagnation than intentional branding.
  • Alignment: Alignment is basic and sometimes awkward. Elements are loosely structured in columns and lists, but the lack of visual refinement makes the layout feel cluttered and unbalanced.
  • Proximity: Proximity is one of the weaker points. Links and categories are densely packed, with minimal spacing or visual grouping, making it hard to scan or find what you need quickly.
